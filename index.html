Interview with Adam Dupuis

  1. How did the iDeck come to be?
    Adam: I found an old Mac Mini my father was using as a monitor stand, and thought, "hey, this would make a great gaming device.", and the rest is history.
  2. Where did you learn to do/make these things?
    Adam: I am in the iCan program. We dedicate about 2 hours at the end of each day to learn about technology, in all ways.
  3. What were you doing previously?
    Well, I had work to do in iCan. We made all sorts of things in photoshop, we were working on a card game, and learning about logic gates through the medium of "Minecraft".
  4. What is the iDeck?
    The iDeck is the premere handheld gaming device on the market. You can play your games, anywhere, anytime. any game.
  5. Why did you think that Apple needed to release a product like this?
    It's simple. There wasn't a gaming-focused product in Apple's lineup yet. So I made one.
  6. Why would one want to join the iCan program?
    If you are interested by comuters in any way, shape, or form, and you get "good grades", you want to join iCan. Trust me.
  7. What skills did you learn that helped make the iDeck possible?
    I learned how to use Linux, the operating system on the iDeck. I learned how to install other operating systems, Everything I needed to know, I was taught or learned myself thanks to iCan.
  8. What inspired you to enter the iCan program?
    I liked computers. Not the act of "gaming" on them (although I do enjoy that too), but the box itself doing the computing. Thought it was cool, I had good grades, so off I went.
  9. What career paths do you think the iCan program will allow you to pursue?
    Many, many things. Animator, Programmer, Engineer, anything. If there's a job that has anything remotely to do with computing, iCan has given me the tools nessecary to do it.
  10. Did you recieve help?
    Nope. Nah. Uh-uh. False. Definitely not. I did not recieve any help from one "Loen [REDACTED] [REDACTED]". No. Where did you get that idea?
